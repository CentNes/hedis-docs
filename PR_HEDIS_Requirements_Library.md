### 2. Plan Vital / Government Health Plan (GHP) MCO contract — quality provisions

The governing instrument is the **Plan Vital MCO Model Contract** (re-procured under RFP "PSG 2022"; new contract term began **January 2023**, with a 2023 update that revamped the quality-incentive program). Four Medicaid MCOs are contracted: **First Medical Health Plan (FMHP), MMM Multi Health, Plan de Salud Menonita (PSM), and Triple-S Salud** (Molina appears in older strategy documents). Contract term references below are drawn from the Model Contract as summarized in the CMS-approved Quality Strategy.

| Provision | Type | What it requires | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Required measure sets (HEDIS / CAHPS / Core Sets)** | Contract + regulation (primary) | MCOs must report a **specific set of HEDIS measures selected/communicated by ASES each calendar year**, plus the **full CMS Adult and Child Core Sets**, EPSDT reporting, and CAHPS. Compliance with 42 CFR 438.340(b)(3)(i). Measure domains cover diabetes (HbA1c testing/control/poor control >9%, BP control, eye exam, KED kidney health eval), controlling high BP, breast/cervical cancer screening (BCS, CCS), well-child (W30) & well-care (WCV), prenatal/postpartum (PPC), oral evaluation, follow-up after mental-illness hospitalization (FUH 7/30-day), diabetes screening for patients on antipsychotics (SSD), ambulatory/preventive care visits, and BH/PH inpatient utilization. | PR Medicaid Managed Care Quality Strategy, Final 10-21-24, §§ on Performance Measures & Quality Strategy Initiatives; 42 CFR 438.340(b)(3)(i). docs.pr.gov → …/PR Medicaid Quality Strategy Final 10-21-24.pdf |
| **CAHPS / member-satisfaction surveys** | Contract (primary) | MCOs must field, at least annually: **CAHPS Health Plan Survey Child version** (incl. Medicaid + Children-with-Chronic-Conditions supplemental items), **CAHPS Health Plan Survey Adult version**, and the **CAHPS ECHO Survey** (behavioral health). A separate **Provider Satisfaction Survey Report** is also required (no prescribed instrument). | Quality Strategy 10-21-24, "Member and Provider Satisfaction" section. |
| **Quality withhold / pay-for-performance — "Health Care Improvement Program" (HCIP)** | Contract (primary) | The HCIP is a **financial withhold through a "retention fund"**: MCOs earn incentive dollars back by hitting ASES benchmark targets on measures grouped into **three initiative categories** — (1) **Healthy People** (preventive screening, 11 quality measures), (2) **Chronic Conditions** (diabetes, asthma, severe heart failure, hypertension, COPD, chronic depression; 16 quality measures), and (3) a third initiative. All HCIP measures are **reported quarterly**. The HCIP replaced the older Disease Management approach and makes MCOs financially responsible for outcome measures. | Quality Strategy 10-21-24, "Health Care Improvement Program" section (¶701, Table 2 HCIP Initiatives). |
| **Withhold amount (2% of administrative fee)** | Secondary (federal summary) | Puerto Rico withholds **2% of the contractor's administrative fee**, earned back quarterly based on performance indicators (HEDIS + satisfaction). *Flag: the exact percentage appears in the CMS Medicaid managed-care profile, a secondary summary; the current Model Contract / HCIP schedule is the authoritative figure and should be confirmed directly.* | "Managed Care in Puerto Rico," Medicaid.gov MCP profile (medicaid.gov/…/downloads/puerto-rico-mcp.pdf). Note: this PDF returns 403 to automated fetchers; retrieve via browser. |
| **Performance Improvement Projects (PIPs)** | Contract + regulation (primary) | Per 42 CFR 438.330(d), MCOs must conduct PIPs. **Minimum required topics:** (1) clinical — kidney health evaluation (KED) to detect early decreased kidney function; (2) clinical — depression/anxiety/SUD screening using nationally-recognized tools; (3) clinical — diabetes outcomes; (4) administrative — EPSDT; (5) administrative — reverse co-location/co-location and integration of physical & behavioral health. MCOs submit proposed PIPs annually for ASES review/approval as part of the QAPI program description; ASES + the EQRO validate them (CMS Protocol 1, 42 CFR 438.340(b)(3)(ii)). *Older documents cited "four or more PIPs"; the 2024 Model Contract lists five minimum topics.* | Quality Strategy 10-21-24, PIP section (¶753–760); 42 CFR 438.330(d), 438.358. |
| **QAPI program** | Contract + regulation (primary) | MCOs must operate a Quality Assessment & Performance Improvement program (annual QAPI program description) with incentives to meet HEDIS standards, deliver preventive services, and reduce unnecessary ED use. | 42 CFR 438.330; Quality Strategy 10-21-24. |
| **Reporting cadence / platform** | Contract (primary) | HCIP quality measures reported **quarterly**; HEDIS + Adult/Child Core Sets reported annually per ASES's CY selection; PIP descriptions annually; geographic network-access reports quarterly. Reports are submitted through the ASES **Enterprise Systems (ES)** platform / **Comprehensive Oversight and Monitoring Program (COMP)** dashboards; results also surface in MCPAR reporting and EQRO reports. | Quality Strategy 10-21-24, monitoring/oversight sections (¶1202–1239). |
| **Plan Vital Reporting Guide** (RFP Attachment 12) | Contract attachment (primary) | Defines the full catalogue of MCO reports, formats, frequencies and deadlines — the operational companion to the measure requirements above. | "Plan Vital Reporting Guide" v.09 (6-22-22), RFP PSG 2022 Attachment 12: docs.pr.gov/…/PSG 2022/Attachment-12-Reporting-Guide-V.09-6-22-22.pdf |

*How the Model Contract itself is obtained:* the executed contract and its attachments are published through the **PSG 2022 RFP package on docs.pr.gov** (ASES → Contratación Gubernamental → Request For Proposals (RFP) → PSG 2022) and referenced in the Quality Strategy. The full contract body is large and distributed across RFP attachments rather than a single public PDF.

### How to obtain the authoritative sources

- **docs.pr.gov** — ASES document library, organized as: `ASES → Publicaciones → Cartas Normativas & Circulares` (policy letters); `ASES → Contratación Gubernamental → RFP → PSG 2022` (Model Contract + Reporting Guide + attachments); `ASES → Monitoreo & Auditoría → Monitoreo → …Quality Strategy` (QS + evaluations); `ASES → Monitoreo & Auditoría → Auditoría → EQRO` (EQR technical reports). Note: docs.pr.gov intermittently fails DNS/timeouts on automated fetchers but downloads reliably via `curl`/browser.
- **bvirtualogp.pr.gov** — free official PDFs of Ley 72-1993 and amendments (OGP legal library); LexJuris (lexjuris.com) mirrors amendment laws free; vLex is paid.
- **medicaid.pr.gov & salud.gov.pr (PRMP / PRDoH)** — EQRO RFP and Medicaid Enterprise System procurement (EQRO is procured by PRMP/PRDoH, not ASES).
- **medicaid.gov** — CMS-side profile, EQR directory, and quality resources (some PDFs block scripted fetch; use a browser).
- **ases.pr.gov** (Regulatory Reports section) — TOC policy, regulatory filings. Do **not** use the defunct/redirecting asespr.org.

**Licensed / not-freely-public flags:** the executed Model Contract body is distributed piecemeal across the PSG 2022 RFP package rather than as one clean public PDF; the exact **HCIP withhold percentage and per-measure benchmark schedules** live in contract attachments/annual Cartas Normativas that must be pulled individually. The 2% administrative-fee withhold figure comes from a **secondary CMS summary** and should be confirmed against the current contract. vLex's annotated Ley 72 is paywalled (the government PDFs are free equivalents).

**Primary/authoritative vs. secondary:** Primary = Ley 72-1993, 42 CFR Part 438 provisions, the Plan Vital Model Contract + Reporting Guide, Cartas Normativas, the EQRO RFP, and the EQR technical reports. Secondary/analytical = the Quality Strategy document (CMS-required but descriptive — excellent as a map, but cite the underlying contract/regulation for binding requirements), QS evaluations, and the Medicaid.gov MCP profile.

## NCQA HEDIS Technical & Audit Requirements — Reference Compilation

*Reference compilation, not legal advice. "Authoritative/primary" = published or governed directly by NCQA (the standard-setter) or by CMS/CFR. "Secondary" = vendor/consultant/state explanations. Licensing/paywall status is flagged per item. All measurement-year (MY) references follow NCQA's convention (e.g., MY 2026 data reported in 2027).*

> **Premise correction (flag):** The request labels "Volume 5" as the *value set directory*. That conflates two separate NCQA products. **HEDIS Volume 5 is the *Technical Specifications for the HEDIS Compliance Audit*** (the audit standards). ### Authority / primary-vs-secondary notes
- **Primary/authoritative** = the statute text on `bvirtualogp.pr.gov` (Oficina de Gerencia y Presupuesto "Biblioteca Virtual") and `lexjuris.com`. ### 1. CMS External Quality Review (EQR) — the EQRO protocols

The federal framework: **42 CFR Part 438, Subpart E** requires states to contract with an External Quality Review Organization (EQRO) and to use CMS's standardized EQR protocols. CMS most recently updated the protocols in **February 2023** (adds the network adequacy protocol; aligns with the 2021 Quality Strategy Toolkit). Puerto Rico's Medicaid managed care program is subject to Subpart E, so its plans are reviewed against these protocols.

### Key flags & caveats
- **Licensed / paid (not publicly downloadable):** HEDIS Volumes 3 and 5, and the digital dQM/DCS measure packages — all purchased/subscribed via NCQA. These are the *normative* audit and survey standards, so budget for them.
- **Contract-specific / not public:** The PR **ASES ("Vital"/Plan de Salud del Gobierno)** managed care contract and PR Medicaid Quality Strategy set the plan's specific reportable measures, PIP topics, and targets. Obtain directly from ASES; they layer on top of everything above.
- **Automated-fetch note:** The `medicaid.gov` PDF links (EQR protocols, protocol standalones, network-adequacy factsheet, CAHPS fact sheet) return HTTP 403 to scripted requests (Akamai bot protection) but are the canonical CMS download URLs surfaced by search and resolve normally in a browser. All NCQA and GitHub links returned HTTP 200.
- **Primary vs secondary at a glance:** Authoritative/primary = 42 CFR Part 438 (Subpart E, §438.330, §438.360); CMS 2023 EQR Protocols; NCQA HEDIS Volumes 3 & 5; NCQA program pages (Compliance Audit, DAV, CAHPS certification, Digital HEDIS); HEDIS FHIR IG. Secondary (context only) = vendor/blog explainers (Myers & Stauffer, Smile Digital Health, Innovaccer, Health Catalyst, IMAT) — useful background, not to be cited as the requirement.
